Tom and Jerry's Cheese Chase

by: Bryan Nguyen

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I felt that the simplicity of this concept engages better than my other ideas. I thought about a game about cat and mouse, and the first characters coming to mind were the television characters Tom and Jerry. They were a perfect fit, and ultimately I decided to pick this concept instead of a maze style game.

2. What I Learned

User testing taught me that there could always be a way to improve your design, even if it seems great to you. For example, I thought my game screen looked great already, but one tester found it quite bland, leading me to include a background from the Tom and Jerry show. Another tester couldn't figure out how to start the game, so I added a set of instructions. There is always another angle that people view your game from, and user testing is how to discover those hidden perspectives.

3. Architecture of My Game

There are 3 main parts of my game: Cheese, Tom, and Jerry. The mouse is controlled by the player, and the objective is to pick up cheese, simultaneously evading the cat. Health starts at 100, whereas score begins at 0. The cat randomly moves around the play space, bouncing off walls in random directions. If the player is touched by the cat, then health is deducted.

4. Technical Implementation

Figuring out how I wanted the cat to move was difficult. Originally, I wanted the cat to bounce off a wall, move in a random direction and speed. As I started to build this part, I realized that it was too difficult to create since I'd have to worry about every angle it could take. I decided to simplify, having the object bounce off a wall into 3 different directions. That way, there are only 3 possible outcomes, rather than the myriad of angles that a random bounce could take.

5. Most Challenging Part

The most challenging part of creating this game was focus. I often found myself drifting off to other activities, then snapping back. This game shouldn't have taken more than 5 classes, but it did. I eventually realized how much time I was wasting, then completed this game quickly.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

I would like to polish up the cat's movement in the future, adding the real random directional movement like I originally wanted. If I were to continue working on this game, I would make it look nicer, since it's pretty raw now.
